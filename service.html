SEO process

1.Know the universe of the client.

Make an "X-ray" of the company for which you are going to work, which will provide you with data such as the objectives and goals pursued with the strategy, what your requirements are, what you want to promote more within your offer, their marketing history (if there is) and any relevant details.

2.Study your environment and current situation.

Once the general history of the client is known, the situation that surrounds it begins to be traced. Study the keywords and analyze the competing companies and determine objectives.

3.Execution of the SEO strategy.

This phase includes the action plan and operations. The designed strategy is launched.

Our Services

SEO techniques

Web architecture optimization

A very important task in SEO is the optimization of the architecture of a website. This architecture is the way in which the contents of the web are organized and distributed.

keyword research

Something that everyone who is dedicated to SEO has to master is the study of keywords. Knowing how to position a website in the first positions of Google is as important as knowing how to search for the right keywords to position that website.

Link creation and optimization

Links are references made on other websites to the web page we are working on. Links are an important part of web positioning, and that is why we have to know very well which sites we can create links on and which links we should create.

Prevention and solution of negative SEO attacks

Negative SEO attacks are actions carried out by some people to lower the ranking of other websites.

Prevention and solution of sanctions

One of the problems that all websites face is Google penalties. Google has quality guidelines in terms of content, advertising, links, etc. If Google finds that a website does not comply, it could impose a penalty, which would drastically reduce the visibility of that website.

Improved user metrics

User metrics are something that is becoming more and more important. User metrics are how users react to our website, whether users stay on our website, when they log in they close the website and go to another website, etc.
